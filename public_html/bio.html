MuKha Fine Arts

About the artist


Mu Reyes Wiesel, who uses the working artist name MuKha, is a Brooklyn and Hawaii-based painter, photographer and multi-media artist. As a painter and photographer not limited to work in the studio, she collaborates with musicians, composers and dancers in projects ranging from improvisational performances onstage to producing multi-media events involving video and musical presentations.

The artist name MuKha comes from the Sanskrit word for “face.” Following the intent of many unnamed artists from indigenous cultures, Mu is guided by a host of influences, both external and internal, from nature and happenstance, feeling more like a co-creator of her works rather than an individual producing art that bears her signature. An ongoing project “Angsudden Song Cycle” with multi-instrumentalist Mike McGinnis produced an LP album based on her paintings and poems, and currently reincarnating into a dance drama by Davalois Fearon Dance.

Born in the Philippines in 1961 and raised in San Francisco from 1970, Mu moved to New York in 1989. While working her career as a journalist and editor in New York City, she continued to develop her art. She studied briefly at the Art Students League and is mostly self-trained. She currently divides her time between Brooklyn, NY and Hanalei Bay, Kauai. MuKha has exhibited her works in the U.S., Europe, and Asia as a visual artist navigating the borderless world of 21 st century art.
